## Session theme
Boast and Drive — treating the 2-wall boast as a genuine attacking option off a loose ball, and
learning to read and punish it when it shows up.

## Warm-up: Boast Touch (10 min)
A feeds a soft ball into B's back corner; B plays a gentle 2-wall boast (side wall then front
wall) into the opposite front corner, just to build racket feel for the angle — no pace, no target
pressure yet. Swap roles and sides after each has had a turn.

## Drill details

### Drill 1: Two-Wall Boast to Length
**Setup:** 2 players, feeder back, hitter also starting back corner. For a third player, rotate
them in as feeder every 3 reps while the hitter stays on to build boast reps across multiple
feeders; swap the hitter role every 2 sets. For a fourth player, run two pairs on Drill 1 and
Drill 2 in parallel on the same court in alternating 10-minute blocks (one pair active, one
resting/watching) rather than splitting the court width, since the boast's diagonal flight path
needs the full court.
**Pattern:** Feeder feeds a loose/short ball into the hitter's back corner. Hitter plays an
attacking 2-wall boast (side wall, then front wall, landing short in the opposite front corner).
Feeder must move in and retrieve it, then plays a straight length drive back to reset. Hitter
recovers to T between reps. Run 8-10 reps each side then swap.
**Coaching points:**
- Boast comes off a short/loose ball, not every ball — this drill teaches the trigger, not a default shot.
- Hit the side wall first at a flat, low angle so it doesn't balloon.
- Recover to T immediately, the boast is not a shot to admire.
**Success criterion:** 7 of 10 boasts land in the front quarter without hitting the tin, and the
hitter is back at the T before the feeder's retrieve arrives.
**Regression:** Feeder feeds a slower, higher, more obviously "boastable" ball; widen target to
the whole front third.
**Progression:** Feeder feeds a tighter, less obviously loose ball, forcing the hitter to judge
for themselves whether a boast is really on.
**Duration:** 20 min.

### Drill 2: Boast-Drive Combo (Boast Counter)
**Setup:** Same pair, live pattern. For a third player, rotate them in as feeder every 3 reps
while the hitter stays on to build boast reps across multiple feeders; swap the hitter role every
2 sets. For a fourth player, run two pairs on Drill 1 and Drill 2 in parallel on the same court in
alternating 10-minute blocks (one pair active, one resting/watching) rather than splitting the
court width, since the boast's diagonal flight path needs the full court.
**Pattern:** Feeder drives normally; whenever the ball comes back loose or short, hitter boasts it
attackingly (as in Drill 1); feeder must scramble forward to retrieve the boast and counters with
either a drop or a straight drive — hitter must read which and recover accordingly.
**Coaching points:**
- Hitter should only boast genuinely loose balls, not force it off a good length ball (that's a bailout, not an attack).
- Feeder counters with intent, not a panicked retrieve.
- Both players stay balanced through the exchange rather than lunging.
**Success criterion:** Hitter correctly identifies and converts at least 4 of 6 "loose ball"
opportunities into a boast that draws a scrambled feeder response.
**Regression:** Feeder tells the hitter out loud when a ball is genuinely loose/boastable,
removing the judgement call while the shot itself is grooved.
**Progression:** Feeder mixes genuinely tight balls in with loose ones at random, hitter must
resist boasting the tight ones.
**Duration:** 20 min.

## Games details

### Games 1: Boast Counter (conditioned, 15 min)
**Condition:** Every rally must include at least one boast from either player before it can be
won — a rally ending without one is a let, replay. If a player boasts everything, dial the
condition back to "at least one boast per game" instead of per rally.
**Scoring:** Straight games to 9, win by 2.
**Rotation:** N/A — single pair on court; see Coach's notes for 3/4-player rotation.
**Duration:** 15 min.

### Games 2: Open Play (normal, 15 min)
**Condition:** None — full rules, applying everything from the session.
**Scoring:** Games to 11 PAR.
**Rotation:** N/A — single pair on court; see Coach's notes for 3/4-player rotation.
**Duration:** 15 min.

## Court split
This session is written for 2 players on 1 court. The 3- and 4-player adaptations live inline
within each drill's Setup above (and apply equally to the games block by rotating the resting
player in between games) rather than as a separate parallel session.

## Coach's notes
- The boast should read as a genuine attacking option off a loose ball, not a habitual escape shot
  off a good length ball — if a player boasts everything in Games 1, dial the condition back to
  "at least one boast per game" instead of per rally.
- With 3 players, keep the hitter on court across multiple feeders to maximize boast reps; with 4
  players, run the two drills in parallel in alternating blocks rather than splitting the court
  width, since the boast's diagonal flight path needs the full court.
