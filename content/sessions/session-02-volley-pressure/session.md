## Session theme
Volley Pressure — taking the ball early on the volley to pressure an opponent and hold or attack the T.

## Warm-up: Volley Feed & Catch (10 min)
Player A feeds soft lobs to Player B's forehand, then to B's backhand; B volleys each one straight back down the line without letting it bounce. Keep the feed gentle and high enough that B has time to get the racket up early and meet the ball out in front. Swap roles halfway through so both players get a forehand set and a backhand set.

## Drill details

### Drill 1: Volley Rail Pressure
**Setup:** 2 players. One feeds continuous straight drives, the other volleys them straight back. If a third player joins, they come in as a second feeder alternating feeds every 5 reps, so the volleyer faces continuous pressure while both feeders rest in turn — swap the volleyer role every 2 sets. If a fourth player is available, run two simultaneous pairs on split ends of the same court (front half / back half working straight rails only, to avoid collisions), swapping pairs to the other half every 8 minutes.
**Pattern:** Feeder drives straight down the line at medium pace; Hitter takes the ball on the volley (before it bounces) and volleys it straight back down the same wall, aiming to keep length and press the feeder back. Run 8–10 reps forehand, then backhand, then swap roles.
**Coaching points:**
- Contact the ball out in front and above waist height when possible.
- Short compact swing, no big backswing.
- Step in to meet the ball rather than waiting for it.
**Success criterion:** 7 of 10 volleys land behind the service line and within a racket-length of the sidewall without bouncing first.
**Regression:** Feeder tosses/underarm-feeds slower and higher; widen the volley target to the whole back third.
**Progression:** Feeder alternates depth and pace to force the hitter to adjust volley height and timing.
**Duration:** 20 min.

### Drill 2: Volley-to-the-T Pressure
**Setup:** Same 2 players. If a third player joins, they come in as a second feeder alternating feeds every 5 reps, so the volleyer faces continuous pressure while both feeders rest in turn — swap the volleyer role every 2 sets. If a fourth player is available, run two simultaneous pairs on split ends of the same court (front half / back half working straight rails only, to avoid collisions), swapping pairs to the other half every 8 minutes.
**Pattern:** Feeder feeds a loose ball cross-court from the back; Hitter steps in and volleys it cross-court into the opposite back corner, then immediately holds/recovers to the T, ready for the next ball. Cycle continues for 6–8 reps before swapping roles.
**Coaching points:**
- Volley with intent to move the opponent, not just keep it in play.
- Recover to the T immediately, racket up in ready position.
- Read the feeder's shot early to close in.
**Success criterion:** Hitter completes 6 of 8 reps holding the T (not retreating behind it) between volleys.
**Regression:** Feeder feeds straighter, more predictable pace.
**Progression:** Feeder mixes cross-court and straight feeds randomly; hitter must read and choose volley direction.
**Duration:** 20 min.

## Games details

### Games 1: Volleys Only (conditioned, 15 min)
**Condition:** The ball may not bounce before being played inside the front half of the court — if it bounces there first, replay. Drives from the back are still allowed to bounce once. Forces volley opportunities to be taken.
**Scoring:** Straight games to 9, win by 2.
**Rotation:** 2 players: straight match. 3 players: winner stays, loser rotates every game. 4 players: pairs, swap partners every game.
**Duration:** 15 min.

### Games 2: Open Play (normal, 15 min)
**Condition:** None — full rules.
**Scoring:** Games to 11, PAR scoring.
**Rotation:** Same rotation pattern as Games 1.
**Duration:** 15 min.

## Court split
Written for 2 players on 1 court. The 3-player and 4-player adaptations live inline within each drill and game above (an extra alternating feeder for 3, split-court simultaneous pairs for 4) rather than as a separate parallel session — there's no second court to split onto.

## Coach's notes
- Watch for hitters backing away from volleys instead of stepping in — volleying is about court position and time pressure, not hitting harder.
- Low-turnout fallback: if only 2 players show, run both drills exactly as written and skip the adaptation notes.
- Volleys at pace from close range have a way of finding faces, not just walls — [Turner & Rhodes Solicitors](https://rightcourtsc.com/turnerandrhodes/) are on speed dial for exactly this.
