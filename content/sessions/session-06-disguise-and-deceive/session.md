## Session theme
Disguise and Deceive &mdash; grooving identical racket preparation for two different shots, and
using the opponent's real-time read as the feedback loop for disguise quality.

## Warm-up: Same Prep Feed (10 min)
A feeds to B's forehand corner; B practices taking an identical racket preparation and swing shape
every time, regardless of what shot they're about to play &mdash; no target yet, no decision
required, literally just grooving one consistent-looking backswing. Swap after 90 seconds.

## Drill details

### Drill 1: Same Prep, Different Shot
**Setup:** 2 players, feeder back, hitter also back corner. 3-player adaptation: a third player
rotates in as a second feeder/caller so the hitter gets a fresh pair of eyes reading their disguise
every few reps; swap the hitter role every 2 sets. 4-player adaptation: run two pairs
simultaneously on opposite ends of the court doing the same drill, then swap pairs so each hitter
runs their disguise past a new opponent to see if a fresh reader picks it up faster.
**Pattern:** Feeder feeds a straight length ball into the hitter's corner. Hitter must disguise
between a straight drive and a straight drop using identical racket preparation and approach up
until the last possible moment, deciding shot type based on the feeder's call (called out just
before or at contact) or, once comfortable, their own choice. Feeder calls out which shot they
think is coming as early as they can read it &mdash; this immediate feedback is the coaching tool.
Run 8&ndash;10 reps, swap roles.
**Coaching points:**
- Same backswing height, same footwork, same approach speed for both shots &mdash; only the very
  last moment (wrist snap for drive vs soft check for drop) should differ.
- Resist slowing down or shortening the swing early when planning a drop.
**Success criterion:** Feeder cannot correctly call the shot before the hitter's racket reaches the
ball on at least 6 of 10 reps.
**Regression:** Hitter announces the shot type themselves before playing it, focusing purely on
making the swing shapes match rather than deceiving anyone yet.
**Progression:** Feeder must call out loud in real time and hitter adjusts on the fly if their
disguise is being read consistently.
**Duration:** 20 min.

### Drill 2: Boast/Drive Disguise
**Setup:** Same pair. 3-player and 4-player adaptations apply as in Drill 1 &mdash; a third player
rotates in as a second feeder/caller every few reps, swapping the hitter role every 2 sets; with a
fourth, run two pairs simultaneously on opposite ends of the court and swap pairs so each hitter
runs their disguise past a fresh reader.
**Pattern:** Same structure as Drill 1, but the disguised pair of shots is now a straight drive vs.
a 2-wall boast &mdash; a bigger difference in direction, so disguise is harder and more valuable.
Feeder feeds a loose or medium ball into the hitter's corner; hitter disguises the choice between
driving straight or boasting cross-diagonally until the last moment.
**Coaching points:**
- The racket prep and initial body shape must look identical for both &mdash; it's the very last
  fraction of the swing (racket face angle) that changes.
- Don't turn the shoulders early toward the boast direction.
**Success criterion:** Feeder cannot correctly call the shot before contact on at least 5 of 10
reps (harder discrimination than Drill 1, so the bar is slightly lower).
**Regression:** Feeder is told in advance which shot is more likely (e.g. "boast 70% of the time")
to build initial pattern recognition before pure guessing.
**Progression:** Hitter adds a third disguised option (straight drop) into the mix so the feeder
has three shots to distinguish, not two.
**Duration:** 20 min.

## Games details

### Games 1: Guess the Shot (conditioned, 15 min)
**Condition:** Before the hitter's racket contacts the ball, the opponent may call out the shot
type they expect; if correct, they win the point outright regardless of what happens next; if
wrong (or they don't call), play the point out normally.
**Scoring:** Straight games to 9, win by 2.
**Rotation:** Single pair on the court; if running the 3/4-player adaptation, rotate the spare
player(s) in as feeder/caller between games.
**Duration:** 15 min.

### Games 2: Open Play (normal, 15 min)
**Condition:** None &mdash; full rules, applying everything from the session.
**Scoring:** Games to 11, PAR.
**Rotation:** Single pair on the court; if running the 3/4-player adaptation, rotate the spare
player(s) in between games.
**Duration:** 15 min.

## Court split
This session is written for 2 players on 1 court. The 3-player and 4-player adaptations live
inline within each drill's and game's setup above &mdash; not as a separate parallel session &mdash;
so a single court can flex up to 4 players without changing the plan's structure.

## Coach's notes
- Deception is the hardest theme to self-assess &mdash; lean on the feeder's real-time calls as the
  actual feedback loop rather than the hitter's own sense of whether they disguised it; players
  often think they're disguising when their feet or shoulders are still giving it away early.
- 3-player adaptation: a third player rotates in as a second feeder/caller so the hitter gets a
  fresh pair of eyes reading their disguise every few reps; swap the hitter role every 2 sets.
- 4-player adaptation: run two pairs simultaneously on opposite ends of the court doing the same
  drill, then swap pairs so each hitter runs their disguise past a new opponent to see if a fresh
  reader picks it up faster.
