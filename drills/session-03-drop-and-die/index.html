Session 3 — Drop and Die

Theme: Drops 2 players (3–4 adaptable) — see Coach's notes 1 court 80 minutes

Session theme

Drop and Die — disguised straight drops off a length ball, and the live drop/retrieve exchange that follows.

Warm-up: Soft Hands Feed & Drop (10 min)

Player A feeds a gentle short ball to Player B's forehand front corner; B plays a soft touch drop back into the opposite front corner, focusing purely on racket-face control and wrist softness, not pace. Swap after ~90 seconds each side.

Drill details

Drill 1: Straight Drop from Length

Setup
2 players, one feeder one hitter, drilling from the back corner. With a third player, rotate them in as a second feeder/retriever every 4 reps so the hitter faces continuous drop reps while feeders swap rest, and rotate the hitter role every 2 sets. With a fourth player, split into two pairs and run this drill on one half of the court while the other pair works Drill 2 on the other half, swapping patterns halfway through the block.
Pattern
Feeder drives a length ball into the hitter's back corner (a normal rail feed). Hitter reads the ball and, instead of driving it, plays a soft, disguised straight drop down the same wall into the front corner. Feeder retrieves and re-feeds. Run 8–10 reps forehand, then backhand, then swap roles.
Coaching points
  • Same racket prep as a drive — don't telegraph the drop early.
  • Contact the ball out in front with an open racket face and soft wrist.
  • Check the swing late, don't "stab" at it.
Success criterion
7 of 10 drops land in the front quarter of the court within a racket-length of the side wall, without hitting the tin.
Regression
Feeder feeds from mid-court instead of full length, giving more time to prepare; widen target to the whole front third.
Progression
Feeder occasionally feeds a shorter, faster ball to force a quicker decision and disguise under time pressure.
Duration
20 min.

Drill 2: Drop & Retrieve Combo

Setup
Same pair, live continuation. With a third player, rotate them in as a second feeder/retriever every 4 reps so the hitter faces continuous drop reps while feeders swap rest, and rotate the hitter role every 2 sets. With a fourth player, split into two pairs and run this drill on one half of the court while the other pair works Drill 1 on the other half, swapping patterns halfway through the block.
Pattern
Feeder feeds length as in Drill 1; hitter drops it short. Feeder must now run in and retrieve the drop (low, controlled pickup) and play it back straight or boast; hitter recovers to the T after their drop and must deal with whatever comes back — a live 2–3 shot pattern, not a dead feed-and-reset.
Coaching points
  • Hitter recovers to T immediately after dropping, racket up.
  • Feeder retrieves with a low knee bend and controlled swing, not a rushed slap.
  • Call "let" honestly given tight front-court proximity.
Success criterion
Rally sustains at least 3 shots (drop, retrieve, response) on 6 of 10 attempts without a tin or skip.
Regression
Feeder allowed to catch and hand-feed the drop instead of racket-retrieving it, to keep the pattern going while retrieval technique develops.
Progression
Hitter must alternate straight and cross-court drops, feeder must vary their response (straight length or boast) each time.
Duration
20 min.

Games details

Games 1: Drop or Die (conditioned, 15 min)

Condition
A rally can only be won by a shot landing in the front quarter of the court (a "kill") — anything else that would normally end a rally is a dead ball, replay.
Scoring
Straight games to 9, win by 2.
Rotation
2 players: straight match. 3 players: winner stays, loser rotates. 4 players: pairs, swap partners every game.
Duration
15 min.

Games 2: Open Play (normal, 15 min)

Condition
None — full rules.
Scoring
Games to 11, standard PAR scoring.
Rotation
Informal.
Duration
15 min.

Court split

Written for 2 players on 1 court. The 3-player and 4-player adaptations live inline within each drill's Setup and within Games 1's rotation above — there's no separate parallel session structure for larger groups.

Coach's notes

  • Watch for hitters using the same pace/swing shape as their drive right up until contact then breaking the wrist late — if the drop is telegraphed early it's not doing its job.
  • Low-turnout: if only 2 players, run both drills exactly as written.
  • A lunge for a dying drop is how good knees go bad — Turner & Rhodes Solicitors handle the aftermath.
